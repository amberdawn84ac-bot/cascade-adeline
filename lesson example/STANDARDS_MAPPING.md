# Standards Mapping Guide

## Overview

This document maps Dear Adeline lessons to Common Core State Standards (CCSS), Oklahoma Academic Standards (OAS), and standard high school course credits for transcripts.

## The Dawes Act Lesson - Complete Standards Mapping

### Common Core ELA Standards

#### Reading Standards for Literacy in History/Social Studies (Grades 6-8)

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RH.6-8.1** - Cite specific textual evidence to support analysis of primary and secondary sources
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** Primary Source blocks require students to cite specific evidence from Dawes Act text, Cherokee family photo analysis, enrollment applications
- ✅ **Assessment:** Investigation prompts ask "Find the phrase about 'surplus' land" and quote specific passages

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RH.6-8.2** - Determine the central ideas or information of a primary or secondary source
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** Students identify main purpose of Dawes Act through document analysis
- ✅ **Assessment:** Quiz asks about stated vs. actual goals of legislation

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RH.6-8.6** - Identify aspects of a text that reveal an author's point of view or purpose
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** Investigation prompts examine who created documents and why
- ✅ **Assessment:** "Who benefits from this policy?" investigation

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RH.6-8.8** - Distinguish among fact, opinion, and reasoned judgment in a text
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** Compare government's stated purpose vs. documented outcomes
- ✅ **Assessment:** Analyze difference between policy claims and actual results

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RH.6-8.9** - Analyze the relationship between a primary and secondary source on the same topic
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** Compare original Dawes Act text to firsthand accounts from Sarah Swimmer
- ✅ **Assessment:** Synthesis questions connect multiple sources

#### Reading Standards for Literacy in History/Social Studies (Grades 9-10)

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RH.9-10.1** - Cite strong and thorough textual evidence
- ✅ **Advanced Path:** Students cite multiple documents to support follow-the-money investigation

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RH.9-10.3** - Analyze in detail a series of events described in a text
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** Timeline of land loss 1887-1934, cause-and-effect analysis

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RH.9-10.9** - Compare and contrast treatments of the same topic in several primary and secondary sources
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** Compare government documents, photographs, and personal applications

#### Writing Standards for Literacy in History/Social Studies (Grades 6-8)

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.WHST.6-8.1** - Write arguments focused on discipline-specific content
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** Investigation worksheet requires claim + evidence about who benefited
- ✅ **Assessment:** Reflection prompts ask students to argue position on policy

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.WHST.6-8.2** - Write informative/explanatory texts
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** Investigation summary worksheet
- ✅ **Assessment:** Document analysis responses

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.WHST.6-8.4** - Produce clear and coherent writing
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** All written response blocks require organized thinking

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.WHST.6-8.7** - Conduct short research projects
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** "Follow the money" investigation = research project
- ✅ **Assessment:** Students research railroad/oil companies

**CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.WHST.6-8.9** - Draw evidence from informational texts to support analysis
- ✅ **Lesson Blocks:** All investigation prompts require evidence from primary sources

### Oklahoma Academic Standards (OAS)

#### Oklahoma History (Required for Graduation)

**OAS.OK.5.2** - The student will examine the settlement and development of Oklahoma
- ✅ **Lesson Content:** Dawes Act directly relates to Oklahoma settlement and land run context
- ✅ **Primary Sources:** Oklahoma Territory documents, Cherokee in Indian Territory

**OAS.OK.5.3** - The student will analyze the relationships among Oklahomans and the land
- ✅ **Lesson Content:** Land allotment policy, agricultural impact, sovereignty issues
- ✅ **Hands-On Activity:** Land division experiment shows practical impact

**OAS.OK.5.4** - The student will identify major Native American tribes and their cultural contributions to Oklahoma
- ✅ **Lesson Content:** Cherokee Nation focus, cultural impact of allotment policy
- ✅ **Primary Sources:** Cherokee language barriers, traditional vs. imposed systems

#### U.S. History Standards

**OAS.USH.3.5** - Evaluate the impact of expansion and economic growth on Native Americans
- ✅ **Lesson Content:** Economic analysis of land loss, "follow the money" investigation
- ✅ **Primary Sources:** Quantitative data on 90 million acres lost

**OAS.USH.2.3** - Analyze the development of government policy toward Native Americans
- ✅ **Lesson Content:** Complete analysis of Dawes Act as government policy
- ✅ **Primary Sources:** Original legislation, implementation documents

### College Board AP U.S. History Framework

For students planning college applications:

**Period 6: 1865-1898 - Key Concept 6.2.I.A** - Federal policy toward Native Americans
- ✅ **Lesson Aligns:** Dawes Act is specifically mentioned in AP framework
- ✅ **Skill Development:** Historical causation, periodization, contextualization

**Historical Thinking Skills**
- **Causation:** Students analyze causes and effects of Dawes Act
- **Continuity and Change:** Compare pre- and post-allotment Cherokee life
- **Contextualization:** Understand Dawes Act within broader westward expansion
